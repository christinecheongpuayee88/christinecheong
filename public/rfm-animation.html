The Foundation

What Does RFM Stand For?

Three dimensions that reveal exactly how valuable each customer is

R
Recency
How recently did they buy?
Recent buyers are more likely to purchase again — the product is still top of mind.
F
Frequency
How often do they buy?
Frequent buyers are your loyalists — they've already proven they love what you offer.
M
Monetary
How much do they spend?
High spenders drive your revenue — and are most likely to spend again in the future.

Key Takeaways

1
Segmentation moves you from mass to personalised marketingSpeak directly to each customer group's needs instead of sending the same message to everyone.
2
RFM scores customers on three behavioural dimensionsRecency, Frequency & Monetary — together they reveal your most (and least) valuable customers.

Best customers score 555 — worst score 111The 5×5 grid gives you 25 segments to act on. Focus on Champions & At-Risk first.
